2016 China-US Symposium: Common Ground

The China-US Symposium is a three day academic conference that aims to foster relations between the twon countries and increase awareness of the importance of working with China to address global problems. This year's China-US Symposium will take place in April, 2016 at the Flecther School of Law and Diplomacy. It will seek to highlight the theme of "Common Ground", or the ways in which the interest of China, its regional neighbos, and the United States overlap.

The symposium is free and open to all. Please register here.

The David Rawson Memorial Lecture: The Future of Security Cooporation in East Asia

David Rawson Memorial Speaker: Randall Phillips

Randal Phillip is Managing Partener for Asia of the Minz Group, and correpondingly Manages the Mintz Group's activities across Asia.
Randy spent 28 years with the Central Intelligence Agency's National Clandestine Service, most recently serving as the Chief CIA representative in China. He has an extensive background in foreign field operations, policy and program management, and leadership development. He is fluent in Mandarin Chinese and Bahasa Indonesia.
